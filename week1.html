Week One written by efeelaiho on June 14, 2015

Week One

I have been waiting patiently to finally take Software Engineering. Every registration this class fills up without fail. As an aspiring software engineer I knew I had to take this course before I graduate, especially since I've been to several final round interviews for Software Engineering Intern positions at top companies, including Twitter and Facebook, but fell short of receiving an offer. Hopefully the skills I will obtain from this class will make me a better candidate.

The syllabus seems really intimidating, but I am more than eager to learn all the concepts Downing has to offer. I already had some experience with Git/Github from a previous internship at a startup, but never had much experience with unit testing and continuous integration.

For anyone debating on whether or not to take this course, do it! Downing is sheer brilliance, and I find his lectures nothing but rewarding. Downing uses the Socratic method of teaching, in which you are assigned material to review over, and during class he'll call on people randomly to not only answer questions, but to also stimulate their critical thinking. This style of teaching is used at several law schools across the nation, so for those future patent attorneys, get used to it. I for one love this method of teaching. I'm usually the guy in the back of the class who dozes off every now and then, but now I wouldn't even dare. The anticipation of being called upon is overwhelming, and definitely keeps me on my toes, even after a giant meal of Chick-fil-A.
